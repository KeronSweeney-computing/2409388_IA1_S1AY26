Personal Development Reflection

Book: Twelve Pillars by Jim Rohn & Chris Widener (Chapters 4–7, pp. 43–80). Below are my chapter summaries and reflections on how I can align these lessons with my life.

Chapter 4 — Achieve Your Goals

Summary + how I will apply it.

Summary

This chapter emphasizes setting clear goals and writing them down. Goals give direction, motivate action, and help measure progress over time. Instead of “hoping,” successful people decide what they want and work toward it consistently.

My Reflection (How I apply it)

  • I will write 3 academic goals for this semester (grades, projects, and attendance).
  • I will break each goal into weekly tasks (study time, practice, revisions).
  • I will review progress every Sunday night and adjust my plan.

Chapter 5 — The Proper Use of Time

Time management + priorities.

Summary

This chapter teaches that time is a resource we can’t get back. Growth happens when we plan, avoid distractions, and put important tasks first. Small actions daily build big results.

My Reflection (How I apply it)

  • I will use a weekly timetable to balance school, work, and rest.
  • I will study in focused blocks (example: 45 minutes study, 10 minutes break).
  • I will reduce time-wasting habits by setting limits on social media.

Chapter 6 — Surround Yourself with the Best People

Influence, mentors, and choosing your circle.

Summary

The chapter explains that the people around us influence our mindset, habits, and success. Being around motivated and positive people helps us aim higher and stay disciplined. Mentors also speed up growth because they guide you away from mistakes.

My Reflection (How I apply it)

  • I will work with classmates who take school seriously and support each other.
  • I will look for a mentor (lecturer, senior student, or professional) for guidance.
  • I will protect my peace by limiting negative influences.

Chapter 7 — Be a Life-Long Learner

Continuous growth and skill-building.

Summary

This chapter highlights that learning must never stop. The world changes fast, and people who read, practice skills, and stay curious improve their opportunities. Education isn’t just school—it’s a lifestyle.

My Reflection (How I apply it)

  • I will read at least 15–20 minutes daily (books, tech articles, course notes).
  • I will practice coding and database concepts consistently, not only before exams.
  • I will build small projects to prove my skills and grow my portfolio.
